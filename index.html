The Truth About Lie Detectors

A storytelling photo exhibit exploring the psychology behind polygraphs what they measure, the myths that surround them, and the human cost of misunderstanding deception.

Exhibit Statement

The Truth About Lie Detectors is a visual storytelling project inspired by the “Deception and Polygraph” lecture in PSYC 2400. This work applies the course concept of physiological deception detection, showing how bodily arousal is often mistaken for proof of lying. Each photograph pairs imagery with an accessible explanation to help viewers understand what polygraphs truly measure, why they produce false results, and how their perceived accuracy impacts real people. The exhibit blends science and art to encourage critical reflection on truth, technology, and the psychology of belief.
